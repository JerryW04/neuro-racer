End your stroke near its start for a lap-able loop — or anywhere else for a point-to-point sprint to the finish flag. Cars keep their brains when you draw a new track.

How it works: each car is driven by a tiny neural network — five distance sensors and its speed go in, steering and throttle come out. Nobody programs the driving: a genetic algorithm breeds the best performers of each generation with random mutations, and crashing ends a car's run. Fitness is progress along the track. Everything runs live in your browser in plain JavaScript — no libraries, no server.
